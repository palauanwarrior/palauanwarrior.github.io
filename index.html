ELIM ×

Its name, “ELIM,” simply means companion. It carries the spirit of Ochaeu, the eagle ray fish and the bird which are known around Palau as natural escorts. In stormy nights the Ochaeru bird lights up the way and guides a lost traveler at sea back to safety. The eagle ray fish emits florescent light just beneath the surface of the sea, leading night travelers away from danger and back to safety.
The eagle ray fish is a very peaceful and gracious sea creature. But, once injured or bothered it retaliates in full and decisive force. This is the trait of a Palauan escort warrior.

ELIM

Palauan War Club

In ancient Palau, this war club was a weapon of choice for warriors of special force who were mostly engaged in escort missions. In those days, high ranking chiefs did not travel to other villages without security escort for fear of getting beheaded along the way.
This weapon gained its fame after it was used by Ngirabins, Warlord of Ngriil Village, Ngerchelong State, to escort High Chief Ibedul of Koror from Idid to Nnermid, Koror.
