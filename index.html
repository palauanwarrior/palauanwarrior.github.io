ELIM

$100 | Oak | 4 Week Carving Period

In ancient Palau, this war club was a weapon of choice for warriors of special force who were mostly engaged in escort missions. In those days, high ranking chiefs did not travel to other villages without security escort for fear of getting beheaded along the way.
This weapon gained its fame after it was used by Ngirabins, Warlord of Ngriil Village, Ngerchelong State, to escort High Chief Ibedul of Koror from Idid to Nnermid, Koror.

MARQUESAS ISLANDS WAR CLUB

$150 | Papaya Tree | 5 Week Carving Period

The U’u club from the Marquesas islands is one of the holy grails for collectors of Oceanic Tribal Art. The clubs are carved from a dense hardwood. There is very little variation in the shape or form of this type of club. If it looks substantially different to the one in the photos it is probably a later and much less valuable example.

These clubs were not just a weapon. they were a status symbol. An object that defined your status as chief. It is because it is a status symbol that so much work and superb craftsmanship is placed in a single weapon.
